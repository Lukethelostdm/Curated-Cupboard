Services

I offer professional decluttering and organising support to help people make sense of their homes and the things within them. The work is shaped around how you live, what feels difficult at the moment, and what will make everyday life easier over time. Support can focus on physical spaces, paperwork, or digital environments, depending on what you need.

Decluttering Support

Decluttering support helps you work through belongings with clarity and confidence. I support decision-making with care and clear guidance, helping you reduce what you no longer need or use without rushing or pressure.

This support is particularly helpful if you:

  • feel stuck or unsure where to begin
  • feel overwhelmed by the volume of things
  • want to make changes without regret

Decluttering is always collaborative and handled at a pace that supports steady progress.

Organising for Everyday Use

Once clutter is reduced, I help organise what remains so your space is easier to use day to day. The focus is on where things live, how they’re accessed, and how they’re put away, based on how you naturally move through your home.

This may involve:

  • grouping items by use rather than category
  • improving visibility and access
  • simplifying storage so it’s easier to maintain

The aim is to reduce daily friction and make routines feel more straightforward.

Storage Planning and Space Use

Where storage is part of the problem, I help plan how space can be used more effectively. This may involve rethinking layouts, identifying where storage is not working effectively, or advising on solutions that support how the space needs to function.

This support can include:

  • assessing what storage is genuinely needed
  • advising on organising products and internal storage
  • planning fitted storage where appropriate

Any installation work is arranged by the client, after which I return to complete the organising work.

Paperwork and Digital Organisation

Clutter isn’t always visible. I also support clients with paperwork and digital organisation, helping bring structure to areas that are often quietly overwhelming.

This may include:

  • sorting and categorising household paperwork
  • creating simple filing systems
  • reviewing digital folders, photos, or inboxes
  • setting up structures that are easy to keep up with

As with physical spaces, systems are kept practical and tailored to your habits.

Ongoing and Follow-Up Support

Homes and routines change over time. Follow-up support is available to refine systems, adjust to new routines, or work through areas that were not addressed initially.

This is particularly helpful during:

  • moves or downsizing
  • family or work changes
  • periods of illness or recovery
  • gradual, phased organising projects

Spaces This Support Can Apply To

Rather than working by fixed room types, support is shaped around what feels most difficult or most important to you.

This may include kitchens, wardrobes, living areas, home offices, storage spaces, or a combination of areas across the home.
